Frequently asked questions

What are the game rules? 📜

- Each player will have 12 kubes.
- Out of these 12 kubes, 7 must be chosen to be placed on the board to start.
- You can place any kube on any starting tile (called a door) you wish.

What's the aim of the game? 🤷🏾‍♂️

- The goal of the game is to move your kubes through the enemy doors.
- Doors have different values ranging from 1 to 3 points, with the point value displayed on the gate. 💯
- To win the game, you must score at least 5 points. 🏆

Who starts ?

The starting player is determined randomly 🔄
On each turn, you can undertake one of the following three actions: Move♟️ Reveal 🎉 and Attack 💣

Move kubes on the board ♟️

- Every kube can move by one tile (⬅️, ➡️, ⬆️, or ⬇️).
- The tile a kube moves into must be empty unless it's passing through a gate to score points, in which case it will exit the board and the player will win points. 🏅
- Two exceptions to this rule are the ⭐ STAR and ☄️ COMET, which move differently when their power is active.

Reveal your kubes! 🎉

To gain the strategic upper hand, players can reveal a kube's hidden ability. 🧠
Upon revealing, the opponent becomes aware of the kube's capabilities, preparing for potential shifts in tactics. 💪
Once a kube is revealed, it stays so until the end of the game.
Typically, after revealing a kube, it cannot be moved during the same turn except for some cases. 🚫

Activate a kube power. 💣

To use a kube's ability, you first need to reveal it to your opponent. 🎯
You can't reveal a kube and move it on the same turn. ❌

However, there are some exceptions to this rule: 🚫
- The eclipse 🌖 kube can be revealed and use its power in the same turn.
- The star ⭐ and comet ☄️ cubes can be revealed and then moved in the same turn.
